Overview of FRC Programming

Where To Start

Robots consist of many different parts called subsystems which each perform their own role for the robot. These are things like the drive, pick-up, and vision functions. After subsystems come the smaller components that they're made of such as motors, servos and sensors which act as the basic electrical components for the robot's functions.

You may want to start right away on the whole robot, but without a knowledge of programing the components, you can't even begin.

WPILib and CTRE

Thankfuly, you don't have to start from scratch. WPILib and CTRE provide various prebuilt libraries that do the dirty work for you and simplify interfacing with hardware such as motors and sensors. Whenever attempting to program a component, it's important to look at the type of motor you're using, and search for it in either the WPILib or CTRE phoenix documentation, depending on the hardware.
